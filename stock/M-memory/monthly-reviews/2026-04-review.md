## Key Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1: Agent 4 Threshold Is Set Wrong
**Evidence:** 16 out of 24 rejected trades (Agent 4 PASS) were actually winners.

**Root cause:** The R:R threshold for entry approval was set above 2.0 or required Sharpe > 1.5, but:
- MA Crossover setups often have R:R of 1.2–1.8 (still +EV)
- Connors RSI setups can have tight stops (1–2%), making raw R:R look small

**Action for May:** Lower Agent 4 threshold from 2.0 to 1.5; allow entries with minimum Sharpe of 0.6.

---

### Lesson 2: "Mixed" Macro Regime Should Not Mean "STAND DOWN"
**Evidence:** 7 stand downs from 04-09 to 04-30; markets were tradeable (volatility was moderate, not crisis-level).

**Root cause:** Macro filter treats "MIXED" as "unclear," which triggers full restriction. But a MIXED regime simply means:
- Long setups need tighter filters
- Short setups need stronger confirmation
- *Some trades should still execute*

**Action for May:** Redefine macro filter logic:
- RISK-ON: Allow all strategies
- NEUTRAL: Require 2/3 agent confirmation
- MIXED: Allow only high-Sharpe setups (>0.8)
- RISK-OFF: Only defensive trades (VIX-related, puts)
- *Never use outright STAND DOWN unless VIX > 40 or circuit breaker*

---

### Lesson 3: MA Crossover Is Your Best Performer
**Evidence:** 11 confirmed wins from 15 signals = 73% accuracy.

**Best setups:**
- Trend reversals (10 EMA crosses 50 EMA after retracement)
- 3–5 day scalps on large-cap names
- 2-week holds on mid-cap value stocks

**Action for May:** Increase MA Crossover allocation from 20% to 35% of signals.

---

### Lesson 4: Connors RSI(2) Works Better on Smaller Position Sizes
**Evidence:** 8 confirmed wins on mean-reversion setups; these had tighter stops (1–2%).

**Finding:** The strategy is sound, but R:R looks bad because:
- Entry: Stock down 5–8% (oversold)
- Target: +1–2% reversion
- Stop: -1% (tight)
- Ratio: 1.5–2.0

With 1% position sizes and 70% win rate, expectancy is still **+1% per month**.

**Action for May:** Use Connors RSI with position sizing of **0.5–1%** (not 2%).

---

### Lesson 5: Speed to Execution Matters
**Evidence:** SPY and QQQ hit targets within **1 day** of entry signal.

**Finding:** The system flags good setups but by the time decision nodes evaluate them, the move has already happened. This suggests:
- Order execution latency is too high
- Daily reviews are too slow
- Intraday rescreening is needed

**Action for May:** Implement **same-day order execution** for high-probability setups flagged before 11 AM ET.
